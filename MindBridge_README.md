## Overview

MindBridge is a mobile app and responsive website designed to fill a critical gap in the mental health support landscape — the space between "I'm not okay" and "I need a therapist." It provides safe, anonymous, free peer support through moderated community circles, flexible mood tracking, and always-visible crisis resources.

This project was designed end-to-end as a UX portfolio piece covering research, competitive analysis, wireframing, visual design, and interactive prototyping across both mobile and web platforms.

---

## The Problem

The mental health app market offers two extremes — expensive clinical platforms with licensed therapists (£65–100/week), and generic wellness apps with no genuine human connection. The vast middle ground is completely empty, leaving three major user groups underserved:

- **Pre-clinical users** — experiencing real anxiety, burnout, or grief but not yet at a clinical threshold
- **Caregivers** — one of the highest burnout groups, almost entirely invisible in consumer mental health design
- **High-stigma communities** — users for whom cultural barriers prevent any formal help-seeking

---

## The Solution

A safe, anonymous, free peer support platform built around five design pillars:

1. **Anonymous by default** — no email required, username only
2. **Moderated peer circles** — topic-based communities with trained peer supporters
3. **Flexible mood tracking** — any cadence, no streaks, no guilt
4. **Always-visible crisis support** — persistent in navigation, never buried
5. **Peer supporter badge pathway** — structured, safe way to give back

## Design Process

### Empathize
Secondary research using published academic literature on digital mental health interventions, community forums, and existing UX research on trust in mental health apps. Developed 5 detailed personas representing:

| Persona | Age | Role | Core need |
|---|---|---|---|
| Aisha Rahman | 24 | Graduate Student | Stigma-free anxiety support |
| Marcus Thompson | 31 | Teacher | Low-friction habit tools |
| Elena Vasquez | 45 | Caregiver | Burnout-specific community |
| Jin-ho Park | 19 | University Student | Cultural safety, anonymity |
| Priya Nair | 38 | Peer Advocate | Structured giving back |

### Define
**Problem Statement:** Young adults, working professionals, and caregivers experiencing mild to moderate mental health challenges need a safe, accessible, stigma-free platform to connect with peers and access self-help tools — but existing apps are either too clinical, too expensive, or require daily commitment incompatible with unpredictable lives.

### Ideate
Competitive audit of 5 apps (Calm, Headspace, BetterHelp, 7 Cups, Woebot). Key finding: no competitor combines peer community, mood tracking, and crisis support in a single free and anonymous product.

### Prototype
- Lo-fi wireframes: 18 mobile screens across 7 flows
- Hi-fi prototype: Full color system, DM Sans typography, Violet #5B4B8A + Sage #4CAF7D palette
- Responsive website: 12 screens across 6 flows

### Test
Moderated usability testing with 5 participants. Identified 3 critical P0 issues:
- Peer circle entry point invisible on home screen (0/5 found it unaided)
- Anonymous sign-up not perceived as genuinely private (4/5 expressed distrust)
- Crisis button undiscoverable (0/5 found it unaided)

## Key Design Decisions

**1. Anonymous-first identity**
No email required at any point. Username + optional avatar only. Users see how they appear to others before committing — building trust before engagement.

**2. Crisis in global navigation**
Crisis support is a persistent element in the bottom navigation bar — present on every screen. Moved from settings to treat safety as a core feature, not a utility.

**3. Designed for non-engagement**
No streaks. No daily obligation. Mood tracker runs on any cadence. Removes guilt for caregivers and users with unpredictable schedules.

**4. Sage Calm color palette**
Deliberately avoids clinical aesthetics. Warm purple and sage green were consistently described by test participants as "calming" and "safe" before reading any content.
